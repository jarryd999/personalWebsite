About Me

People ask me why I like programming, and I always come back to the same analogy: it's like solving a really hard sudoku problem; frustrating while you're working through it, but an extreme wave of satisfaction when you get through with it. I get that feeling working on a lot of different projects, I find that the same types of problems tend to arise on all platforms/languages/frameworks/etc. As a result, I'm language agnostic and have come to consider myself a generalist. I did, however, do some specialty work with computational linguistics. Actually, a lot of the same problems faced there are shared when building a compiler, both of which I found to be really interesting problems to work on!


Aside from programming, I'm really into music and sports. I've had a lifelong struggle rooting for New York teams (Jets, Mets, and Nets), even though the first two have found some recent success. As a lifelong soccer fan, Arsenal and Barcelona have made up for all the blown games and seasons I've witnessed with my other teams. As for music, I have a really wide range in taste. Check out my SoundCloud or Last.fm accounts to get a feel for what I'm into. It's always been about the live experience for me though, nothing can match the energy of the crowd and the artist feeding off that; a good live performer creates this feedback cycle that is really exciting and unmatched by any other listening experience!
